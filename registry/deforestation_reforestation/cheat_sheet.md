FACILITATION CHEAT SHEET — Deforestation Reforestation

TIMING (50-min period)
  Phase 1: ~12 min  |  Phase 2: ~10 min  |  Phase 3: ~13 min  |  Phase 4: ~15 min

WHAT TO EXPECT
  Flaw 1: "They only talked about the good stuff" — turns 5, 7, and 8 (Sofia)
    Students should notice: Sofia lists benefit after benefit — community involvement, jobs, animals coming back, before-and-after photos — without ever mentioning a single downside, even after Marcus directly asks about limitations
    Most will catch this.

  Flaw 2: "Someone raised a good point but gave up on it" — turns 9, 11, and 13 (Marcus)
    Students should notice: Marcus says 'that's not really honest' and 'they deserve the full picture' in turn 9, then caves in turn 11 saying 'let's just do it your way,' and in turn 13 starts to bring it up again but stops mid-sentence with 'actually, never mind, it's fine'
    Harder to spot.

PHASE 1: Student isn't finding flaws
→ "Re-read turns 5 through 8. Sofia is talking a lot about reforestation. Make a list of all the good things she mentions. Now — did she mention anything bad or any problems? What does that tell you?"
→ "Follow just Marcus's turns from turn 9 to 13. Does he change his mind, or does he just stop arguing? What's the difference?"
→ "Look at turn 4, where Marcus asks a direct question about downsides. Then read Sofia's response in turn 5. Did she actually answer his question?"
→ "Read turn 13 really carefully — what does Marcus start to say, and what does he do next? Why do you think he stopped?"

PHASE 2: Student found flaw but stuck on thinking behavior
→ For "They only talked about the good stuff": Narrow to 3: "They only focused on one part and missed the bigger picture" / "They only paid attention to things that agreed with them" / "They believed it because they felt strongly, not because they had proof"
→ "Imagine you're Sofia. You found these incredible stories about tree planting — communities coming together, whole landscapes turning green. You're pumped about making an amazing presentation. Someone asks you about the downsides. Why might you skip over them and keep talking about the good stuff?"
→ For "Someone raised a good point but gave up on it": Narrow to 3: "They just went along with everyone else instead of thinking for themselves" / "They believed it because of who said it, not because the evidence was good" / "They believed it because they felt strongly, not because they had proof"
→ "Imagine you're Marcus. You believe the audience deserves the full picture, and you said so. But Sofia sounds really confident and excited, and you still have to build the escape room together. Why might you say 'let's just do it your way' even though she never actually proved you wrong?"

PHASE 3: Group discussion is stalled [same for every scenario]
→ "Did you all mark the same turns? Look at where you differ."
→ "Someone in your group found something you missed. Take another look."

PHASE 4: Class isn't engaging with AI perspective
→ "Some of you noticed that Sofia only talks about the good side of reforestation. That's exactly what the AI noticed too. Nice work — now can you point to the specific turns where she does this?"
→ "The AI says Marcus gave up his point because of groupthink — he just went along with Sofia instead of thinking for himself. But some of you might have picked a different reason. If you said something else, make your case — why does your explanation fit better?"
→ "Let's look at turns 11 and 13 together. Marcus agrees with Sofia's plan both times. But look closely — did Sofia ever actually answer his concern about the downsides? What's the difference between being convinced and just giving up?"
→ "Here's a tough one: Sofia says 'I hear you, I do' in turn 10. Does she really hear Marcus? What would it look like if she actually addressed his point instead of just acknowledging it?"
→ "Go back to turn 13. Marcus starts to say something about how 'planting can't keep up with the cutting' and then says 'never mind.' Why do you think he stopped? What does that tell you about how the discussion ended?"
→ "If you caught that Marcus's concern about honesty in turn 9 was never actually resolved — that's a big one. The group decided on a plan, but did they actually work through their disagreement, or did one person just stop pushing?"
